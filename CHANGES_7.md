# TriageIQ — Changes 7: Vitals Chart Rendering

---

## 1. Vitals Sparkline Charts Now Render
**File:** `backend/orchestrator.py`

**What changed:** The `VitalsTrends` and `Sparkline` components existed in the frontend but never rendered because the report was missing the two fields they required — `vitals_raw` and `vitals_trends`. Both fields are now attached to the report in `run_triage_agent()` after synthesis completes:

- **`vitals_raw`** — the patient's structured vitals dict (e.g. `{"bp": "88/60", "hr": 112, ...}`) taken directly from the patient data. This drives the vital sign cards (current value, color coding, unit label).
- **`vitals_trends`** — generated by a new `_generate_trends()` helper that produces six historical readings per vital sign for sparkline display.

`vitals_raw` and `vitals_trends` are only attached when the patient has structured vitals (i.e. demo patient mode). Free-text patients without structured vitals continue to show the text analysis from the vitals agent.

A `import random` was added to the top of `orchestrator.py` to support the trend generation.

**Why:** The vitals tab was silently falling back to plain text for every patient because `report.vitals_raw` was never set. The chart component was fully built and waiting — it just had no data to render.

## 2. Clinically Meaningful Trend Directions
**File:** `backend/orchestrator.py`

**What changed:** The `_generate_trends()` helper generates trend lines that reflect the patient's clinical trajectory, not random noise. Each vital sign is checked against its normal range and assigned a direction:

| Vital | Condition | Trend |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Heart Rate | > 100 (tachycardia) | Rising toward current |
| Heart Rate | < 60 (bradycardia) | Declining toward current |
| Heart Rate | Normal | Stable |
| SpO₂ | < 95% (hypoxia) | Declining toward current |
| SpO₂ | Normal | Stable |
| Resp. Rate | > 20 (tachypnea) | Rising toward current |
| Resp. Rate | Normal | Stable |
| Temperature | > 38.0°C (fever) | Rising toward current |
| Temperature | < 36.0°C (hypothermia) | Declining toward current |
| Temperature | Normal | Stable |
| GCS | < 14 (altered) | Declining toward current (integer-clamped 1–15) |
| GCS | Normal | Flat at 15 |
| BP (systolic) | < 90 (hypotension) | Declining toward current |
| BP (systolic) | > 140 (hypertension) | Rising toward current |
| BP (systolic) | Normal | Stable |

**Why:** Random trend data would look unconvincing and miss the clinical story. A declining BP sparkline on PT-006 (BP 82/50) immediately communicates deterioration. A flat SpO₂ line on PT-003 (SpO₂ 99%) communicates stability. The sparklines now reinforce the ESI score rather than just filling space.
