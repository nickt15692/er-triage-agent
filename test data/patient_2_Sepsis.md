## Free Text Version
Paste this into the Free Text Input tab:

> 71 year old female, arrived by EMS. Confusion onset this morning, family reports she has had a fever and cough for 3 days. BP 88/58, HR 124, RR 26, SpO2 91% on room air, Temp 39.4°C, GCS 12. History of type 2 diabetes, chronic kidney disease stage 3, and recent UTI treated with antibiotics 2 weeks ago. Allergies: sulfa drugs.

---

## What this demonstrates

- **ESI 2 — Emergent** response
- Triggers the Sepsis 3-Hour Bundle Protocol
- Classic sepsis triad: fever + hypotension + altered mental status (GCS 12)
- Multiple critically abnormal vitals hit simultaneously
- Shows the system identifying a less obvious emergency — no single dramatic symptom, but the pattern across vitals and history is life-threatening
- Contrast with Patient 1: similar ESI urgency but a completely different clinical picture

---

## Expected key outputs
- ESI Score: 2
- Care area: Resus
- Interventions: Blood cultures x2 before antibiotics, broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour, lactate level, 30mL/kg IV crystalloid, vasopressors if MAP stays below 65
- Time to physician: Under 10 minutes
